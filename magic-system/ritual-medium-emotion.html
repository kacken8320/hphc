Ritual, Medium and Emotion

In order to perform a spell, the witch or wizard must perform a Ritual. The spell can be cast through a Medium (0 - n). An Emotion can be added to change the strength and/or effect of the spell (depending on the genuine Emotion). Which means that in general, one can perform a charm with only a Ritual; Medium and Emotion are optional.

Ritual

A Ritual is an act, with which a charm is being initiated. The Ritual decides the effect of the spell, as well as the strength. It can be anything a witch or wizard does. The default for most of hphc is: saying a spell, moving a wand, concentrating on something. But it can also be things such as: dancing, singing, making music, maths, calligraphy, building, studying, drawing, dreaming, eating, intoxication, murder, ...

The more complicated and longer the Ritual, the stronger the charm.

Medium

The Medium is a physical object through which the charm is being cast. It can be anything physical. The default for most of hphc is a wand. The more impractical/bulky/unique/valuable the Medium, the stronger the charm.

Emotion

Emotions can be roughly categorized to light and dark. If the wizard performs the spell without a particular Emotion, we call this spell neutral. Further, you can split light and dark Emotions into Emotions felt in connection to other people, or not; of course there is also in-between.
Apart from connected or not-connected Emotions, dark Emotions can further be categorized as being "bad" (harmful, aggressive, outwardly destructive, ...), and "sad" (wounded, heavy, inwardly destructive, ...). A soul will be broken, if one tries to hurt (physically and Emotionally) another with a spell that is modified by a bad Emotion.
The stronger the Emotion, the stronger the spell. Emotions can add additional effects (0 - n) to the spell.

Other

Witches and wizards can't, in general, join their spells. To do so is extremely dangerous and can entangle their minds and bodies. The knowledge on how to properly do it is long lost.
